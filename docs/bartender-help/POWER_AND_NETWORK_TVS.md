# Turning TVs On and Off — Bartender Help

**For:** the bartender working the bar right now.
**Goal:** every TV that should be on is on, every TV that should be off is off, and nothing is showing the wrong input.
**Time to fix:** 5–30 seconds for the everyday stuff. The one slow thing (setting up a brand-new Samsung) takes about 15 minutes and shouldn't be done mid-shift.
**You can't break it:** every step here is the normal way to do this. Nothing in the iPad's Power tab can damage a TV. The wall switch behind the TV is always the last-resort reset and it can't hurt anything either.

Most shifts you'll use the Power tab twice — once at the start of your shift to bring everything up, once at the end to shut everything down. The rest of the time it's for "that one TV that's acting weird." Work top to bottom; the easy stuff is first.

---

## 30-second triage — start here

1. **Opening or closing the bar?** Skip to **bulk power** just below — one button does the whole bar.
2. **Just one TV is the problem** (off when it should be on, on when it should be off, showing the wrong thing)? Skip to **"Just kill that one TV"** or **"TV is on the wrong input."**
3. **Manager asked you to set up a brand-new TV** or re-pair a Samsung that "stopped working"? That's the **pairing** section — a 15-minute job, don't start it during a rush.

---

## Two ways the iPad talks to TVs (you don't need to think about this)

Some TVs are controlled by equipment in the back room (a switcher box and a small black blaster that fires invisible signals, the same way your living-room remote does). Other TVs — mostly the newer Samsungs and the Rokus — talk to the iPad directly over the bar's Wi-Fi.

**The only thing that matters:** you don't have to pick which method. Tap a TV's power button and the system figures it out. Smart TVs respond in about a second; the back-room-blaster ones take 3–5 seconds. That's the only difference you'll notice.

## Opening or closing the bar — bulk power

**What this looks like:** 10 AM, every TV is dark. Or 2 AM, you've kicked the last person out, and you want everything off without tapping 18 power buttons.

**Time:** 5 seconds to press the button, then 5–20 seconds for the TVs to respond.

### What to do

1. Pick up the iPad. Tap the **Power** tab at the top (the tab with the power icon — circle with a line through the top).
2. At the top of the Power tab, find the strip labeled **TV Power Control** with a button on the right: **Toggle All TVs**.
3. Tap **Toggle All TVs** once. The button shows a spinning circle for a few seconds.
4. Don't tap it again. One tap fires power to every TV. Tapping twice turns everything that just came on back off.

### What the customer sees

Opening: TVs come on one by one over 5–20 seconds. Each shows its brand logo for a second, then whatever channel was last on it. Closing: TVs go black one by one over 5–20 seconds. If a customer is finishing their drink, the TV in front of them dies — that's the signal you're closing.

### How you know it worked

Walk the floor and look. Every TV should be on (opening) or off (closing). The Power tab shows a colored dot on each tile: **green** = on, **yellow** = standby, **red** = off.

### If it didn't work

- **Most came on but one or two stayed dark:** normal. Some TVs miss the first wake-up signal. Tap that TV's individual power button (next section). It almost always pops on with one tap.
- **Nothing happened:** wait a full 30 seconds. If still nothing, tap **Toggle All TVs** once more. If still nothing, jump to **"TV won't respond"** below.
- **TVs came on but a couple show static or the wrong source:** that's a Video-tab problem. Open the **Wrong Channel on a TV** doc.

### What NOT to do

- **Don't tap Toggle All TVs over and over.** Each tap reverses the last one. Tap 3 times "to make sure" and half the TVs end up in the wrong state.
- **Don't unplug TVs from the wall as a first move.** The wall is the last resort.

## Just kill that one TV (the back patio is empty, the bulb costs money)

**What this looks like:** the back patio cleared out at 9 PM, nobody's watching, and you want to turn off just those two TVs.

**Time:** 5 seconds per TV.

### What to do

1. Tap the **Power** tab. Scroll to the grid of TV tiles below the bulk-power strip. Each tile has the TV's name at the top ("Patio Left" or "TV 12").
2. Find the tile for the TV you want off.
3. Tap the wide **Power** button in the middle. If the TV is on, the button is **green**; tap and the TV turns off, button turns **red** within a second.

### What the customer sees

The TV goes black within 1–3 seconds. No transition, no logo — just off.

### How you know it worked

TV is dark. Button is red, dot turned green to red.

### If it didn't work

- **Tapped but TV is still on:** wait 3 more seconds. Older TVs are slow. If after 5 seconds still on, tap once more — only once. Rapid taps toggle right back on.
- **Spinning circle for more than 10 seconds:** the system can't reach that TV. Jump to **"TV won't respond"** below.

### What NOT to do

- **Don't tap power 5 times in a row.** Each tap is a toggle. You'll end up where you started.

## TV is on the wrong input (came back from break, TV 3 is showing static)

**What this looks like:** TV 3 was showing the Brewers game before your break. You're back and it's showing a Roku menu, static, or a Netflix loading screen. Someone (a customer with a TV remote, or a glitch) switched the TV's input.

**Time:** 10 seconds.

Quick word on "input": every TV has several places on the back where things plug in — cable box in one, streaming stick in another. Each is an "input" (HDMI 1, 2, 3, 4). Wrong input = the picture is coming from a thing that isn't running, hence the static.

### What to do

1. Tap the **Power** tab. Find the tile for the wrong TV.
2. Near the bottom of the tile, **HDMI Input** sits above four little buttons: **1**, **2**, **3**, **4**.
3. One is highlighted in blue — that's the current (wrong) input.
4. Tap the buttons one at a time, starting with **1**. After each tap walk over and look at the TV. The one showing the game is the right input.
5. Once you find it, stop tapping.

### Which input is the cable box?

Varies TV to TV depending on how it was wired. At most bars the cable box is on **HDMI 1** and the streaming stick on **HDMI 2**, but check with your manager. Once you've found the right input for a TV, it almost never changes — sticky note behind the bar if it helps.

### What the customer sees

The TV blinks black for half a second between inputs, then shows the new source. Worst case, trying all 4 takes 20 seconds and looks like channel surfing. Not a big deal.

### How you know it worked

The game (or channel) is on the TV. The correct input button on the iPad is highlighted blue.

### If you tried all four and none show the right thing

The cable box or streaming stick on that TV might be off, unplugged, or crashed. Open the **Wrong Channel on a TV** doc — Section 3 (static / no signal) and Section 4 (TV dark) have the fix.

### What NOT to do

- **Don't keep tapping inputs after you found the right one.** Once you see the game, stop.

## TV won't respond — is it dead?

**What this looks like:** you tapped a TV's power button 2 or 3 times. Nothing happened. The dot is still wrong. The TV just sits there.

**Time:** under a minute most of the time.

A TV not responding usually doesn't mean it's broken — almost always the signal just didn't reach it. Try these in order.

### Try first (15 seconds)

1. Tap that TV's power button **one more time**. Single tap, then stop.
2. Wait a full 10 seconds — actually count to ten.
3. Look at the TV and the colored dot. Did either change?

If anything moved at all, the system is working — it was just slow.

### If still nothing (next 30 seconds)

Walk to the TV:

- **Any light on the TV at all?** Most TVs have a tiny LED on the front or bottom edge. If you see a small light, the TV has power and is just in standby — try the iPad power button one more time.
- **No light at all?** The TV might be unplugged, or the wall outlet flipped off. Check the wall plate behind/under the TV.

### Last resort — power-cycle at the wall

Only if the iPad didn't work AND there's no light on the TV.

1. Find the wall outlet the TV is plugged into. Sometimes a wall switch controls that outlet.
2. If there's a switch, flip it off, wait 10 seconds, flip it back on. The TV should boot.
3. If there's no switch (just a plug straight into the wall), leave it alone and text the manager.

**Important:** the wall switch is always a safe reset. You cannot damage a TV by cutting and restoring power. TVs are designed for this — power outages happen, the TV is fine.

### What the customer sees

After the wall-switch trick, the TV is dark for 10–20 seconds while it reboots, then lights up and goes back to whatever was on. Anyone watching just sees "huh, that TV blinked." If a customer asks, "it's restarting, give it 30 seconds" is the right answer.

### When to give up

You tried the iPad twice, walked over, checked for lights, tried the wall switch, and the TV is still dark with no lights at all — that TV needs the AV tech. Switch any important game to a different TV using the Video tab, and text the manager.

### What NOT to do

- **Don't keep mashing the iPad power button.** Five rapid taps does the same nothing as one.
- **Don't crawl behind the TV and start unplugging things.** HDMI and power cords look similar in the dark. You can knock something else loose.

## Setting up a brand-new TV / re-pairing a Samsung

**What this looks like:** the manager swapped in a new Samsung today, OR an existing Samsung suddenly says it needs to be re-paired. You see an orange **Pair** button on the TV's tile.

**Time:** about 15 minutes. **Do not start this during a busy shift.**

This is the ONE Power-tab task that asks you to leave the bar. You walk to the TV with its physical remote, accept a permission popup on the TV's screen, and walk back. If the popup times out before you get there, you start over. Do this between rushes or before doors open.

A word on "pairing": when the system first tries to talk to a Samsung over Wi-Fi, the TV is protective — it pops up "the device 'Sports Bar TV' wants to control this TV — Allow / Deny?" Pairing is you walking over and tapping **Allow** with the physical remote. The TV then remembers it forever (unless factory-reset).

### Before you start

- Get the TV's physical remote from the drawer under the register, labeled to match the TV ("TV 7 remote").
- Make sure the TV is **on**. If it's off, turn it on with the regular Power button and wait 15 seconds.

### What to do

1. On the Power tab, find the new Samsung's tile. The orange **Pair** button has a lightning-bolt icon.
2. Tap **Pair**. The button changes to **Waiting for TV...** with a spinning circle.
3. **Walk to the TV right now.** Don't wait — about 30 seconds before the request expires.
4. Within 5–10 seconds, a popup appears: "**Sports Bar TV** wants to connect" with **Allow** and **Deny**.
5. Use the physical remote. Arrow keys to highlight **Allow**, center button (OK or Enter) to confirm.
6. Popup disappears. Walk back. The Pair button is gone, replaced by a green Power button. Paired and ready.

### What the customer sees

Almost nothing. The TV keeps showing the game. The popup is small — a corner-of-screen prompt, not a full takeover. "I'm setting up the new TV, give me a minute" covers it.

### How you know it worked

Orange Pair button is gone, the tile has a green Power button and the HDMI input row, and tapping Power once turns the TV off/on within a second or two.

### If it didn't work

- **No popup appeared:** request timed out OR the TV is on a different Wi-Fi than the iPad. Tap Pair again, walk faster.
- **You accidentally pressed Deny:** no harm. Tap Pair again, walk back, pick Allow.
- **Popup says "another device is already paired":** stale connection. Text the manager — they need to clear the old pairing record.
- **No Pair button on the tile, even though it's a brand-new TV:** the TV hasn't been discovered on the network. The AV tech needs to run a scan. Text the manager.

### What NOT to do

- **Don't start pairing during a rush.** A popup will sit on the TV staring at customers if you abandon it halfway.
- **Don't try without the physical remote.** You can't accept the popup any other way.
- **Don't hit Pair multiple times in a row.** Each tap restarts the request.

## I renamed a TV and now I can't find it

**What this looks like:** earlier you tapped a TV's name and changed it from "TV 7" to "Back Patio Right." Now you're looking for "TV 7" and it's gone.

**Time:** 5 seconds to undo.

TV names on the iPad are just labels. The system tracks every TV by an internal ID you never see, so renaming is always safe — it changes the sticker, not the wiring. Rename as many times as you want.

### What to do

1. Find the tile with the new name. **Tap the name** at the top of the tile — it turns into an editable text box.
2. Type whatever you want and press Enter (or tap the small **OK** button). Change it back to "TV 7" or whatever it was.

The customer sees nothing — iPad-only, the TV itself is unaffected.

## What the red, yellow, and green dots mean

Each tile has a small colored dot next to the brand icon: **green** = on, **yellow** = standby (between on and off — some TVs sit there instead of fully off so they wake faster, that's normal), **red** = off.

**Important catch:** the dots are the system's best guess and aren't always 100% right. If a customer turned a TV on with the wall switch or a stray remote, the system won't know until the next check (within 30 seconds). If you tap a TV and the dot stays the wrong color for more than 30 seconds, look at the TV — your eyes beat the iPad.

If a dot is consistently wrong on the same TV every time (always red even though it's clearly on), tell the manager. Not urgent, but worth tracking.

## When to text the manager

Text the manager if:

- A TV won't respond to the iPad **AND** the wall switch trick didn't bring it back. (Hardware problem — AV-tech territory.)
- Pairing a Samsung failed twice in a row, even with the right popup steps.
- **Toggle All TVs** does nothing at all — not even one TV responds. (The iPad probably lost connection to the back room.)
- Multiple TVs went dark at the same time for no clear reason. Could be a tripped breaker; not your problem to fix.
- A TV has visible physical damage (cracked screen, smoke smell, anything scary). Power it off from the iPad if you can, then leave it alone.

### What to include in the text

1. **A photo of the TV** — even a black screen tells the manager whether standby lights are on, whether anything's visibly wrong.
2. **A photo of the iPad's Power tab** showing that TV's tile with the colored dot.
3. **One sentence:** which TV (name or number), what's happening, what you tried. Example: "Patio Right won't turn on. Tapped Power on the iPad twice, no response. Walked over, no standby light. Flipped the wall switch off and back on — still nothing."

## A few things worth knowing

- **Power tab and Video tab do different things.** Power tab = whether the TV is on/off and which input it's on. Video tab = WHAT is showing on the TV (which cable box, which game, which channel). TV on but wrong game? Video tab.
- **You don't need to power TVs off between games.** TVs are made to be on all day. Only turn one off mid-shift if the area is empty or a customer asks.
- **The floating "Ask AI" button works for power questions too.** Weird message, new icon, behavior you've never seen — tap the floating chat bubble in the iPad's corner and ask in plain English. "Why is TV 5 showing a red dot when it's clearly on?" gets a real answer.

## You did great

Power problems look scary because TVs are big and expensive, but nothing you tap on the iPad can damage anything, and the wall switch is always a safe reset. The manager would rather get a text from you about a stuck TV than discover it cold at the start of the next shift.

Two things to memorize:

1. **Tap once, wait 5–10 seconds, then look.** Patience beats panic.
2. **The wall switch always works.** If the iPad fails, the wall cuts power, and that's safe.

Everything else you can look up here when you need it. You've got this.
