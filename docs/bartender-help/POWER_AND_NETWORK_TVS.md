# Turning TVs On and Off — Bartender Help

**For:** the bartender working the bar right now.
**Goal:** every TV that should be on is on, every TV that should be off is off.
**Time to fix:** 5–30 seconds for the everyday stuff. Pairing a brand-new Samsung takes about 15 minutes — don't do it mid-shift.
**You can't break it:** nothing in the iPad's Power tab can damage a TV. The wall switch behind the TV is always the last-resort reset and it can't hurt anything either.

---

## What the Power tab is FOR

**The Power tab does two things:**

1. **Turn TVs on and off** — single TV or the whole bar at once.
2. **Reboot a network TV** that's gone weird.

**The Power tab does NOT** put games on TVs, change channels, or pick which cable box feeds which TV. **That's the Video tab.** See [[PUTTING_GAMES_ON_TVS_VIDEO_TAB]] for routing.

If a customer asks "can you put the Brewers game on TV 5?" — that's a Video-tab job. The TV just needs to be **on** for the Video tab to do its thing.

---

## 30-second triage

1. **Opening or closing the bar?** Skip to **bulk power** below — one button does the whole bar.
2. **Just one TV is the problem** (off when it should be on, on when it should be off)? Skip to **"Just kill that one TV"**.
3. **Customer wants a different game on a TV that's already on?** Not here. Open **[[PUTTING_GAMES_ON_TVS_VIDEO_TAB]]** or **[[WRONG_CHANNEL_ON_TV]]**.
4. **Manager asked you to pair a brand-new Samsung?** That's the **pairing** section — 15-minute job, between rushes.

---

## Two kinds of TVs (you don't need to memorize this)

Some TVs talk to the iPad directly over Wi-Fi — **network TVs**, mostly Samsungs and Rokus. Others (older sets, projectors, perimeter TVs) are controlled by back-room equipment.

- **Network TVs** show a tile with a Power button, a Reboot button, **and HDMI input buttons** (1, 2, 3, 4).
- **Non-network TVs** show a Power button only — no Reboot, no HDMI.
- **Bars without network TVs never see HDMI buttons at all** — totally normal. Source switching at your bar happens through the Video tab.

Tap a power button and the system figures out which kind. Network TVs respond in about a second; back-room ones take 3–5 seconds.

## Opening or closing the bar — bulk power

**Time:** 5 seconds to press, then 5–20 seconds for the TVs to respond.

1. Tap the **Power** tab at the bottom of the iPad (circle-with-a-line icon).
2. At the top, find the strip labeled **TV Power Control** and tap **Toggle All TVs**. Button shows a spinning circle for a few seconds.
3. Don't tap it again. One tap fires power to every TV. A second tap reverses the first.

**How you know it worked:** walk the floor. Tiles show colored dots — **green** = on, **yellow** = standby, **red** = off.

**If it didn't work:**
- **One or two stayed dark:** normal — some TVs miss the first wake-up. Tap their individual power buttons (next section).
- **Nothing happened at all:** wait 30 seconds, tap **Toggle All TVs** once more. If still nothing, jump to **"TV won't respond"**.
- **TVs came on but show static or wrong source:** that's a Video-tab problem — see [[WRONG_CHANNEL_ON_TV]].

**Don't:** tap Toggle All TVs over and over. Each tap reverses the last one — half the TVs end up wrong.

---

## Just kill that one TV (the back patio is empty)

**Time:** 5 seconds per TV.

1. Tap **Power**. Scroll to the grid of TV tiles below the bulk-power strip.
2. Find the tile with the TV's name ("Patio Left", "TV 12").
3. Tap the wide **Power** button in the middle. Green means on; tap and it turns red within a second.

**If it didn't work:**
- **Still on after 3 seconds:** wait 5 more. Older TVs are slow. Then tap **once** more — only once.
- **Spinning circle longer than 10 seconds:** jump to **"TV won't respond"** below.

---

## Reboot a network TV that's acting weird

**What this looks like:** a network TV (Samsung or Roku) is frozen, stuck on a "loading..." screen, or its menu won't go away. TV is on, just stuck.

**Time:** 60 seconds.

1. On the Power tab, find the TV's tile.
2. Tap the **Reboot** button (next to the Power button). It only appears on network TVs — if you don't see one, this TV isn't a network TV. Use the wall switch (see "TV won't respond" below).
3. Wait 60 seconds. The TV goes dark, then comes back through its startup logo to whatever was on before.

If it comes back but stays frozen, try Reboot once more. If it stays dark, use the wall switch.

---

## HDMI buttons on the tile (network TVs only)

**What this looks like:** a network TV's tile shows four buttons labeled **1**, **2**, **3**, **4** under **HDMI Input**.

**What they're for:** picking which thing on the back of the TV feeds the picture — cable box, streaming stick, etc. **You'll almost never touch these.** The Video tab handles source switching automatically across all your TVs.

**When you actually would use them:** rare — usually because a customer with a stray TV remote changed the input on a specific network TV and the Video tab can't recover it. Then tap **1**, **2**, **3**, **4** one at a time until the right source appears.

**Bars without network TVs never see these buttons** — also normal.

For everyday wrong-source / wrong-channel problems, see [[WRONG_CHANNEL_ON_TV]].

## TV won't respond — is it dead?

You tapped power 2–3 times. Nothing. Dot is still wrong. Almost always the signal just didn't reach it — try these in order.

### Try first (15 seconds)

1. Tap that TV's power button **one more time** — single tap.
2. Wait a full 10 seconds (actually count to ten).
3. Look at the TV and the dot. Did either change?

If anything moved, the system is working — just slow.

### If still nothing

Walk to the TV:
- **Tiny LED on the front/bottom edge?** It has power and is just in standby — tap the iPad power button once more.
- **No light at all?** Might be unplugged or the wall outlet is off. Check the wall plate behind/under the TV.

### Last resort — wall switch

Only if the iPad didn't work AND there's no light on the TV.

1. Find the wall outlet. Some have a wall switch controlling them.
2. Flip the switch off, wait 10 seconds, flip back on. The TV should boot.
3. If there's no switch (just a plug), leave it and text the manager.

**The wall switch is always a safe reset.** You cannot damage a TV by cutting and restoring power.

### When to give up

Tried the iPad twice, walked over, checked for lights, tried the wall switch, and the TV is still dark with no lights — that TV needs the AV tech. Move any important game to a different TV via the Video tab ([[PUTTING_GAMES_ON_TVS_VIDEO_TAB]]) and text the manager.

## Setting up a brand-new TV / re-pairing a Samsung

**What this looks like:** an orange **Pair** button (lightning-bolt icon) on a TV's tile.

**Time:** about 15 minutes. **Do not start during a busy shift.** This is a separate, one-time setup task for a network TV — you walk to the TV with its physical remote, accept a popup on the TV's screen, walk back.

### Steps

1. Get the TV's physical remote from the drawer under the register ("TV 7 remote").
2. Make sure the TV is **on**. If not, use the physical Power button and wait 15 seconds.
3. On the Power tab, tap the orange **Pair** button. It changes to **Waiting for TV...** with a spinning circle.
4. **Walk to the TV right now** — about 30 seconds before the request expires.
5. A popup appears within 5–10 seconds. With the physical remote, arrow to **Allow**, press OK/Enter.
6. Walk back. The Pair button is gone, replaced by a green Power button.

**If it didn't work:**
- **No popup:** timed out or wrong Wi-Fi. Tap Pair again, walk faster.
- **You pressed Deny:** no harm — tap Pair again, walk back, pick Allow.
- **"Another device is already paired":** stale connection — text the manager.
- **No Pair button on a brand-new TV's tile:** the TV hasn't been discovered. AV tech needs to scan — text the manager.

---

## What the red, yellow, green dots mean

**Green** = on, **yellow** = standby, **red** = off.

**Catch:** the dots are the system's best guess and aren't always 100% right. If a customer turned a TV on with a wall switch, the system won't know until the next check (within 30 seconds). If a dot stays wrong for more than 30 seconds, look at the TV — your eyes beat the iPad.

---

## When to text the manager

- A TV won't respond to the iPad **AND** the wall switch didn't bring it back.
- Pairing a Samsung failed twice in a row.
- **Toggle All TVs** does nothing at all — not even one TV.
- Multiple TVs went dark at the same time for no clear reason.
- A TV has visible damage (cracked screen, smoke smell).

**Include:** a photo of the TV, a photo of the iPad Power-tab tile, and one sentence — which TV, what's happening, what you tried.

## Quick reference card

| What you want | Where to go |
|---|---|
| Turn on every TV | Power tab → **Toggle All TVs** |
| Turn off every TV | Power tab → **Toggle All TVs** |
| Turn off one TV | Power tab → tile → **Power** button |
| Reboot a frozen network TV | Power tab → tile → **Reboot** button |
| Switch which game/source is on a TV | NOT here — use **[[PUTTING_GAMES_ON_TVS_VIDEO_TAB]]** |
| Wrong channel, static, "No Signal" | NOT here — use **[[WRONG_CHANNEL_ON_TV]]** |
| TV won't respond | Tap once more, wait 10s → walk over → wall switch as last resort |
| Set up a new Samsung | Power tab → orange **Pair** → walk to TV with remote → press **Allow** |

**Two things to remember:**

1. **Tap once, wait 5–10 seconds, then look.** Patience beats panic.
2. **Power tab turns TVs on/off. Video tab decides what's on them.** Don't hunt for source-switching here.

You've got this.
