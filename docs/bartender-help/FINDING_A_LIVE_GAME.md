# Finding a Live Game Fast — Bartender Help

**For:** the bartender when a customer walks up and asks "is the Bucks game on?"
**Goal:** find the game and get it on a TV in under a minute.
**Time to fix:** about 30 seconds with the Guide tab.
**You can't break it:** tapping Watch on a game just routes it to a TV. If it ends up on the wrong TV, you fix that in another tap. Try things.

---

## When you'd use this

Customer is at the bar right now asking about a specific game. You don't know if it's started, you don't know the channel, you just want to find it and put it up. The **Guide** tab is built for exactly this.

If the game starts later (say, the 7pm Bucks game and it's 4pm now), use **[[SCHEDULING_GAMES_AHEAD]]** instead — you can queue it up so it auto-tunes at tipoff.

---

## Where the Guide tab lives

On the iPad behind the bar, look along the bottom row of tabs. You'll see **Video**, **Guide**, **Music** and a few others. Tap **Guide**.

The Guide tab fills the screen with **tiles, one per live game right now**. Each tile shows:

- The two teams (logos and short names — "MIL @ BOS")
- The league (NBA, NFL, MLB, NHL, CFB)
- The current channel ("Channel 40", "ESPN", "Fox Sports 1")
- The score if the game has started, or the start time if it hasn't
- A small **Watch** button on the right

---

## Step-by-step — finding and routing a game

1. Tap **Guide** at the bottom of the iPad.
2. Scan the tiles for the team the customer asked about. The league label (NFL, NBA, etc.) helps you narrow fast.
3. If there are lots of games, use the search box at the top — type the team name ("Bucks") or the channel number ("40").
4. Found the right tile? **Tap Watch.**
5. A TV picker pops up — tiles for every TV in the bar.
6. Tap each TV that should show this game.
7. Tap **Apply** at the bottom.
8. The Watch button briefly flashes a checkmark. The picked TVs switch within a few seconds.

---

## Reading a game tile

The tile tells you almost everything you need at a glance:

- **Score** (e.g. "MIL 78 - BOS 74, 4th Qtr 3:12") = the game is live right now. Tap Watch and go.
- **Start time** (e.g. "Tips off 7:30 PM") = the game hasn't started yet. Watch will still route the channel, but the box may be on something else (pre-game show, another game). For a not-yet-started game, **[[SCHEDULING_GAMES_AHEAD]]** is actually the better tool.
- **"FINAL"** = the game ended. Tapping Watch will get you the post-game show on that channel, which usually isn't what the customer wants. Ask them what else.

---

## If the game isn't in the Guide tiles

Some games don't auto-show up. The most common reason is the Brewers — Wisconsin's Brewers games are on **Channel 308 (Bally Sports Wisconsin+)**, and the channel-guide injection sometimes lags by a few minutes at game time.

1. Use the search box at the top of the Guide tab — type "Brewers" or "308" or the away team.
2. If nothing matches and you're sure the game is on, switch to manual: go to **[[PUTTING_GAMES_ON_TVS_VIDEO_TAB]]**, pick the TV, tap the cable preset for the right channel.

For Wisconsin sports specifically, here are the channels worth memorizing:

- **Brewers** → Channel 308 (Bally Sports Wisconsin+)
- **Bucks** → Channel 40 (FanDuel Sports Network Wisconsin)
- **Packers (national broadcasts)** → 5 (Fox), 9 (CBS), or 13 (NBC) depending on the week
- **Badgers basketball/football** → check the Guide first; varies by network
- **ESPN** → 206
- **Fox Sports 1** → 219

## What the customer sees

About 2-5 seconds after you tap Apply, the TV they're looking at changes. They might see a half-second of black between sources — that's normal, not a bug. Then the game's picture and sound come through.

If you tell the customer "Bucks are on Channel 40, putting them up now" — that's the right script. You don't need to explain the Guide tab or the routing system. Just confirm the game and the TV.

---

## If this didn't work

- **Watch did nothing — TV didn't change** → wait 5 seconds, then tap Watch and Apply again. Cable boxes can be mid-tune and ignore the first request.
- **Wrong TV got the game** → tap Watch on the same tile again, this time un-select the wrong TV and pick the right one. Tap Apply. The wrong TV will switch back to whatever it was showing within a few seconds.
- **The Brewers game isn't showing in the Guide** → search "308" or "Brewers". If still nothing, the channel-guide feed is behind. Go to **[[PUTTING_GAMES_ON_TVS_VIDEO_TAB]]** and tune Channel 308 manually on the TV.
- **Guide tab is empty or stuck on "Loading"** → swipe down to refresh. Wait 10 seconds. Still empty? The live-game feed is having a moment — fall back to manually routing via Video tab.
- **TV switched but it's the wrong sport** → the cable box hadn't finished tuning. Wait 5-10 more seconds. If still wrong, the channel preset may have hit the wrong number; tap the TV in the Video tab and tap the right preset.
- **Customer asks for a specific channel I don't see in the Guide** (Golf Channel, Fox News, etc.) → the Guide tab only shows live sports. For non-sports channels, use **[[PUTTING_GAMES_ON_TVS_VIDEO_TAB]]** with the preset or the channel pad.

---

## What NOT to do

- **Don't tap Watch multiple times in a row on the same game.** Each tap kicks off a routing request. Wait 5 seconds between attempts.
- **Don't tap Watch on a FINAL game** unless you want the post-game show. Ask the customer if they meant a different game.
- **Don't grab the physical remote on the TV when the Guide tab is doing the work.** The system and the physical remote can fight each other — let the iPad finish, then re-check.
