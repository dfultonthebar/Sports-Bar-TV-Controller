# Wrong Channel on a TV — Fix It Fast

**For:** the bartender working the bar tonight.
**Goal:** get the right game on the right TV in under 90 seconds.
**You can't break anything by trying the steps below.** Every step is safe. If something looks weird, scroll to the bottom for how to text the manager.

---

## First — what's the problem?

Look at the TV the customer is asking about. Answer these two questions:

**1. Is the TV on at all?**
- Screen is totally dark or shows "No Signal" → go to **Section 4 (TV is dark)**.
- Screen is on and showing something → keep going.

**2. Is it showing the wrong thing?**
- Wrong game, wrong channel, or fuzzy static → keep reading, you're in the right place.
- If most/all TVs look wrong at the same time → go to **Section 5 (everything is wrong)**.

---

## The tool you'll use: the iPad behind the bar

Almost every fix below uses the **iPad behind the bar** — the one mounted or sitting near the register that's always showing the bar's TV controls. If the screen is off, tap it once to wake it up. You should see a row of tabs across the top: **Video**, **Guide**, **Music**, and a few others.

If the iPad is dead or missing, see **Section 5** at the bottom — that's a manager problem.

**Quick mental model:** every TV in the bar is hooked up to a "switcher" in the back room. The switcher decides which box (cable, satellite, or streaming) feeds each TV. The iPad is the remote for the switcher. You tell it "put DirecTV box 3 on TV 7" and it does it.

## Section 1 — TV is showing the WRONG SOURCE

**What this looks like:** the customer wants the Brewers game on cable. The TV is on, but it's showing a DirecTV menu, or a streaming app, or a different sport entirely.

**Why this happens:** each TV can show one of several boxes — the cable box, the DirecTV satellite box, or the streaming box (Fire TV). Someone earlier switched TV 4 to the wrong box.

### Try first

Tap the right box for the channel they asked for. Brewers games are usually on cable (channel 308) — so you want **cable** on that TV.

### What to do

1. Pick up the iPad behind the bar.
2. Tap the **Video** tab at the top.
3. You'll see tiles, one for each TV in the bar. Find the tile labeled **TV 4** (or whichever TV the customer asked about).
4. Tap that TV's tile.
5. A list of sources pops up — things like "Cable Box 1", "DirecTV 1", "DirecTV 2", "Fire TV 1", and so on.
6. Tap the source the customer wants. For Brewers on cable, tap **Cable Box 1** (or whichever cable box is listed).
7. The list closes by itself in about half a second.

### How you know it worked

Walk over to TV 4 and look. Within 1-2 seconds, the picture should change to whatever the cable box is currently showing. It might not be the Brewers game yet — that's Section 2. But the source should now be cable.

### If it didn't work

- **Nothing changed on the TV:** wait 5 more seconds. The switcher can be slow if a lot is happening. Then try Section 5.
- **It changed to the right source but the source itself is showing static or "No Signal":** that box is having trouble. Go to **Section 3**.
- **You can't find a tile for TV 4 on the iPad:** the iPad may be on a filtered view. Look for a row of buttons at the top of the Video tab labeled with room names (Main Bar, Patio, etc.) — tap **All** or tap the room TV 4 lives in.

## Section 2 — Right source, WRONG CHANNEL

**What this looks like:** TV 4 is on cable (correct) but it's showing a cooking show, not the Brewers game.

**Why this happens:** the cable box on that TV is tuned to a different channel than what the customer wants.

### Try first

Look on the iPad for a **preset button** for the team the customer asked about. Most regular games (Brewers, Bucks, Packers, Badgers) have a one-tap preset.

### What to do

1. On the iPad, tap the **Video** tab if you're not already there.
2. Tap the tile for **TV 4**.
3. Look for a section labeled **Channel Presets** or a row of colored buttons with team names or logos.
4. Tap the **Brewers** button (or whichever team the customer asked for).
5. The cable box will change channels. Give it 3-5 seconds — cable boxes are slow.

### How you know it worked

Walk over to TV 4. The picture should now show the game the customer asked for. You'll usually see the team logos, the score in the corner, or the broadcaster's graphics ("Bally Sports Wisconsin+" for Brewers, "FanDuel Sports Network Wisconsin" for Bucks).

### If there's no preset button for what they want

1. On TV 4's tile, look for a number pad (buttons 0-9) or a field labeled **Channel**.
2. Tap the channel number from this list:
   - **Brewers:** 308 (Bally Sports Wisconsin+)
   - **Bucks:** 40 (FanDuel Sports Network Wisconsin)
   - **Packers (national):** 5 (Fox), 9 (CBS), or 13 (NBC) — depends on the game
   - **ESPN:** 206
   - **Fox Sports 1:** 219
3. Tap **Enter** or **Send** after typing the number.
4. Wait 3-5 seconds for the cable box to tune.

### If it didn't work

- **Cable box ignored you:** the cable box may be stuck. Go to **Section 3**.
- **You tuned to the right channel but the wrong game is on:** the game might not have started yet, or it's on a different network tonight. Check the **Guide** tab at the top of the iPad — it shows what live games are on which channels right now.
- **Channel pad isn't responding to taps:** try once more. If still nothing, go to **Section 5**.

## Section 3 — Static, "No Signal", or frozen picture

**What this looks like:** the TV is on, the source is right (cable), but the picture is fuzzy static, a frozen image, a black screen with "No Signal", or a colorful "searching for signal" screen.

**Why this happens:** the cable box crashed or lost its connection. This happens a few times a month and is usually fixed by power-cycling the box.

### Important — you're power-cycling the CABLE BOX, not the TV

The cable boxes live on the equipment rack in the back room. They're small black boxes about the size of a large paperback book, usually stacked together. Each has a small light on the front (green or blue when on).

### Try first

Do the safe restart from the iPad before walking to the rack.

### What to do (from the iPad)

1. On the iPad, tap the **Video** tab.
2. Tap the tile for the TV that's showing static.
3. Look for a button labeled **Reboot Box** or **Power Cycle**. Tap it.
4. Wait 60 seconds. The box takes about a minute to come back.
5. Look at the TV. The cable box's startup screen should appear, then the channel should come back.

### What to do (if the iPad doesn't have that button, or it didn't work)

1. Walk to the equipment rack in the back.
2. Find the cable box that feeds the TV you're fixing. The boxes are usually labeled with masking tape — "Cable 1", "Cable 2", etc. — matching the source names on the iPad.
3. **Unplug the power cord** from the BACK of the cable box (not the wall). It's the thickest cord, usually black, going into a round port labeled "Power" or "DC IN".
4. Count to 15 slowly.
5. **Plug the power cord back in.**
6. Walk back out to the bar. Wait 60 seconds. The box's startup screens will play through, then your channel should be back.

### How you know it worked

The TV shows the cable channel (or any cable channel) again with normal picture and sound.

### If it didn't work

- **TV still shows static after the box restarted:** the cable signal to the bar might be out. Try a DIFFERENT TV that's on cable — if all cable TVs show static, the cable company is down. Text the manager.
- **The cable box didn't come back on (no lights):** the box may have failed. Switch the customer's TV to a different source (DirecTV — see Section 1) so they can still watch something, and text the manager.

## Section 4 — TV is dark / totally off

**What this looks like:** the TV is just a black rectangle. No picture, no menus, no "No Signal" message — nothing on the screen at all.

**Why this happens:** the TV is powered off. Either someone shut it off, the bar's power-on routine missed it this morning, or the TV is unplugged.

### Try first — the Power tab

The iPad has a **Power** tab in the bottom row (a circle with a line through the top, next to Video). This is the primary way to turn a TV back on.

1. On the iPad, tap the **Power** tab.
2. Find the tile for the TV that's off. Each tile has the TV's name at the top ("TV 7", "Patio Left", etc.) and a colored dot — **red** means off.
3. Tap the wide **Power** button in the middle of the tile.
4. Wait 5 seconds. The dot should turn green and the TV should light up.

For full Power-tab details (Toggle All TVs, network-TV reboot, dot meanings) see **[[POWER_AND_NETWORK_TVS]]**.

### How you know it worked

The TV's screen lights up. You should see a logo (Samsung, LG, Sony) for a second, then the picture from whatever box was last assigned to it. The dot on the Power-tab tile turns green within 5 seconds.

### If it didn't work — try the physical remote

Some TVs in the bar don't respond to the iPad's Power tab — they're the older or cable-only TVs (perimeter sets, projectors, patio in some bars). For those, use the physical remote:

1. Find the TV's physical remote. They're stored in the **drawer under the register**, labeled with masking tape matching the TV number ("TV 7 remote", "TV 8 remote").
2. Point the remote at the TV (not the iPad).
3. Press the **Power** button (red, usually top-left of the remote).
4. The TV should turn on within 2 seconds.

### If it STILL didn't work

- **TV doesn't respond to either iPad or physical remote:** the TV might be unplugged or have lost power at the wall. Walk to the TV and check: any tiny LED on the front edge? If yes, it has power and is just being stubborn — try the Power tab once more. If no LED, check the wall outlet. The wall switch is always a safe reset (it can't damage the TV) — see **[[POWER_AND_NETWORK_TVS]]** for the wall-switch procedure.
- **TV comes on but immediately goes black:** something is wrong with the source feeding it. Go to **Section 1** and switch it to a different source.

## Section 5 — ALL the TVs are wrong / iPad isn't working

**What this looks like:** every TV in the bar suddenly switched to the wrong thing, OR the iPad isn't responding, OR you can't find your TV's tile.

**This is NOT something you can fix at the bar.** This is a switcher problem and needs the manager.

### What to do right now (to keep customers happy while you wait)

1. For the most important TV (usually the biggest screen with the biggest game), use the **physical remote** for that TV (in the drawer under the register). Tape down the channel they want — even if the iPad's wrong, the TV will keep showing what you tune it to.
2. For other TVs, do the same with their physical remotes if customers are asking.
3. Text the manager (see below).

### What NOT to do

- **Do not unplug anything from the equipment rack.** The black boxes, the silver boxes with the green lights, the small box with the antenna — leave them all alone. Pulling power on the wrong thing can take 30 minutes to recover from.
- **Do not press the reset button** on anything in the rack.

## When to text the manager

Text the manager if:

- A whole section above didn't fix the problem.
- All the TVs are wrong at once (Section 5).
- Cable signal is out across the whole bar.
- A TV won't turn on with either the iPad or the physical remote.
- The iPad is dead, frozen, or you can't find it.
- A customer is upset and you've already tried 2 things.

### What to include in the text

1. **A photo of the TV screen** showing the problem (static, wrong game, "No Signal", whatever it is).
2. **A photo of the iPad screen** showing the TV tile and source list you were looking at.
3. **One sentence:** which TV (the number — "TV 4"), what the customer wants ("Brewers game"), what you tried ("switched it to cable, tuned channel 308, no picture").

### Example text

> Hey, TV 7 won't show the Brewers game. I switched it to Cable Box 2 on the iPad, tuned channel 308 — TV shows static. Tried rebooting the cable box from the iPad and waited 60 seconds, still static. Photo of screen and iPad attached.

That gives the manager enough to know what's broken and what you already ruled out, so they can fix it remotely or tell you the next thing to try.

## Quick reference card (memorize the top 3)

| Situation | First thing to try |
|---|---|
| Wrong source on TV | iPad → Video tab → tap the TV → tap the correct source |
| Right source, wrong channel | iPad → tap the TV → tap the preset for the team they want |
| Static or "No Signal" on cable | iPad → tap the TV → tap **Reboot Box** → wait 60 seconds |
| TV is dark | iPad → **Power** tab → that TV's tile → **Power** button (or use the physical remote in the register drawer). Full Power-tab guide: [[POWER_AND_NETWORK_TVS]] |
| Everything is wrong | Physical remotes for what matters most + text the manager |

**Remember:** you can tap any button on the iPad without breaking anything. The worst that happens is the wrong TV changes for a few seconds — you'll just change it back. Try things.
