# Putting Games on TVs — Bartender Help

**For:** the bartender working the bar tonight.
**Goal:** get the right game on the right TV (or several TVs) without poking around for five minutes.
**Time to fix:** most of these are under 60 seconds once you know where to tap.
**You can't break it:** every step here is a normal thing to do. The worst you can do is put a game on the wrong TV for ten seconds — and you fix that by tapping it again. Try things.

---

## How to think about this page

The tool for almost everything here is the **iPad behind the bar** — the tablet always showing TV controls. Tap to wake it. Along the bottom is a row of icons: **Video**, **Guide**, **Routing**, **Remote**, plus a few smaller ones. Those are tabs.

The three you'll live in:

- **Video** — a layout that looks like the bar's floor plan, every TV drawn where it actually hangs. Tap a TV to change what it's showing.
- **Guide** — live games right now, like a TV listing. Tap a game's Watch button and the system puts it on the TVs for you.
- **Schedule** — games coming up in the next few hours. Pre-assign TVs and the system tunes them at kickoff.

Video tab = **right now on a specific TV**. Guide tab = **find a game fast**. Schedule tab = **game starting soon**.

---

## 30-second triage — start here

Three questions before you tap. The answer tells you which scenario below.

1. **Customer knows what they want, or asking "is the game on"?**
   - Knows the game → Scenario 1.
   - Asking what's on / where → Scenario 2.

2. **Specific TV, or "anywhere"?**
   - Specific → Scenario 1 or 3.
   - Anywhere → Guide tab (Scenario 2) picks for you.

3. **Already playing, or starting later?**
   - Playing now → Video or Guide.
   - Starts in the next hour or two → Schedule (Scenario 3).

## Scenario 1: "Put the Brewers on TV 7 right now"

**What this looks like:** customer walks up, points at one specific TV, says "put the Brewers on that". You know the game and the TV. You just need to make it happen.

**Time to fix:** under 30 seconds.

**What the customer sees while you fix it:** TV stays on whatever it was showing for about 2 seconds, then flips to the game. A half-second of black screen between sources is normal.

### Quick check first

Are you on the **Video** tab? If not, tap **Video** along the bottom. The floor-plan layout fills the screen with every TV drawn at its real wall position.

### What to do

1. Find the TV the customer pointed at — each one's labeled ("TV 7", "Main Bar Left", "Patio Big Screen").
2. **Tap it.** A panel slides up listing every source — cable boxes, DirecTV boxes, Fire TV sticks.
3. Tap the source with the game. Brewers on cable → **Cable Box 1** (whichever one tunes Bally Sports Wisconsin+). Packers on DirecTV → a **DirecTV** source.
4. Panel closes itself. Glance at the TV to confirm.

### How you know it worked

The TV changes to whatever that source is currently showing within a second or two. If the source was already on the right channel, you're done. If it's on the wrong channel, see Scenario 4 to change channels.

### What to say to the customer

"On its way. Give it a few seconds." Don't promise it's the exact game until you've eyeballed the TV — the cable box might still need to change channels.

### If it didn't work

- **TV didn't change:** tap the TV again, tap the source again. Tap firmly — light taps sometimes miss.
- **TV is on the right source but wrong sport:** the box is on the wrong channel — see Scenario 4.
- **You don't see the source you want:** scroll inside the panel. Usually 8-15 sources, they don't all fit.
- **TV is dark:** see **WRONG_CHANNEL_ON_TV.md**, section "TV is dark".

### What NOT to do

- **Don't tap a different TV while the panel is open.** Close it first (X or tap the dim area), then tap the next TV.
- **Don't unplug anything on the equipment rack.** All of this is software.

## Scenario 2: "Where's the [team] game?"

**What this looks like:** customer asks "is the Bucks game on?" and you don't know the channel or whether it's even started.

**Time to fix:** under 60 seconds — the Guide tab does the work.

**What the customer sees while you fix it:** a couple of taps, then a TV changes. Faster than flipping channels with a remote.

### Quick check first

Glance at the purple-bordered **Shift Brief** card at the top of the iPad — it lists tonight's big games and heads-ups. If the team's mentioned, you've got the channel and start time without searching.

### What to do (Guide tab — fastest)

1. Tap **Guide** along the bottom.
2. You'll see tiles, one per live game right now — teams, score, channel, and a small **Watch** button.
3. Scan for the team. The tiles include the league (NFL, NBA, NHL, MLB, CFB) so you can narrow fast.
4. **Tap Watch.** The system tunes the right box to the right channel and pops up a TV picker.
5. Tap each TV that should show the game.
6. Hit **Apply**.

### If the game isn't in the Guide tiles

1. Use the search box near the top — type the team or channel number ("Bucks" or "40").
2. If something matches, tap it — same Watch flow.
3. If nothing matches and you're sure the game is on, it might not have started yet (Scenario 3) or it's on a network the system doesn't track. Fall back to Scenario 1 + Scenario 4 to do it manually.

### How you know it worked

The Watch button briefly shows a checkmark. The TVs you picked switch within a few seconds. A "Routed to Output X" banner at the top clears after three seconds.

### What to say to the customer

If you found it: "Bucks are on Channel 40, putting them up now." If you didn't: "Let me check the start time — might not have tipped off yet."

### If it didn't work

- **Tapped Watch, nothing happened:** the cable box may be mid-tune. Wait 5 seconds and tap again.
- **TV changed but still wrong channel:** the cable box didn't tune. Try Scenario 4 (presets / type the number).
- **Guide tab is empty or stuck on "Loading":** wait 10 seconds, then swipe down to refresh. Still empty → the live-game feed is down. Fall back to typing the channel on the Video tab.

## Scenario 3: "The 4 o'clock game is starting"

**What this looks like:** 3:30 PM Sunday, early NFL games wrapping up, late games about to kick. You want the late games on the right TVs **before** kickoff so customers don't watch the wrong game while you fumble.

**Time to fix:** 2-3 minutes to set up the whole afternoon, then it runs itself.

**What the customer sees while you fix it:** ideally nothing. TVs flip to the new games at kickoff automatically; customers just notice the right game is on at the right time.

### Quick check first

Tap **More** at the bottom (three-dot icon), then **Schedule**. You'll see a list of games scheduled in the next few hours.

### What to do (one game at a time)

1. Find the game — each entry shows teams, start time, channel.
2. Tap **Pick TVs** (or the small TV icon) on that row.
3. A picker pops up showing every TV. Tap each TV that should show this game.
4. Tap **Save** or **Apply**.

The system tunes the right box to the right channel at kickoff. You don't have to be at the bar.

### What to do (AI Suggest — system picks the TVs)

Fast path when six or seven games start at once.

1. Tap **AI Suggest** near the top (sparkle icon).
2. Wait. **90 seconds to 3 minutes.** The system is sorting which game belongs on which TV based on home-team priority, customer patterns, and TV size. Spinner is visible. **Don't tap anything.**
3. When it finishes, you get a list of recommended assignments — "Packers → TV 7, 8, 12", "Bucks → TV 4, 5".
4. Scan them. Usually right. Tap any row to adjust.
5. Tap **Apply All**.

### How you know it worked

Each scheduled game shows a TV count badge ("3 TVs"). At kickoff, the assigned TVs switch automatically. A green status badge briefly flashes at the top when a scheduled tune fires.

### What to say to the customer

At 3:55 PM, customer asks "are you putting the Bears on at 4?" — you can say "yes, it's queued up on TV 6 and TV 9." You're reading directly from the Schedule list.

### If it didn't work

- **AI Suggest spins more than 3 minutes:** local AI is off. Cancel and assign manually.
- **Game started but TV didn't switch:** schedule fires within ~30 seconds of kickoff. Wait a minute. Still nothing → switch manually (Scenario 1) and text the manager.
- **You scheduled the wrong TVs:** tap the game in the list again — the picker reopens with your selections. Adjust and re-save. Works any time, even after kickoff.

### What NOT to do

- **Don't schedule the same game on every TV "just to be safe".** That blocks customers who want something else. 2-4 TVs per game; leave the rest free.
- **Don't tap AI Suggest twice in a row.** Each tap starts a fresh 2-3 minute job. Wait the first one out.

## Scenario 4: "Customer asked for a specific channel"

**What this looks like:** customer says "put on Fox News" or "throw on the Golf Channel" or "ESPN 2, please". You know the channel; you just need to tune it on a TV.

**Time to fix:** under 30 seconds with presets; about a minute if you have to type the number.

**What the customer sees while you fix it:** TV changes within a few seconds. They probably won't even watch you do it.

### Quick check first

First make sure the TV is on the right box: on the iPad tap **Video**, tap the TV the customer is near, and route it to the cable or DirecTV source if it isn't already. Changing the *channel* happens on the **Remote** tab (next).

### What to do (preset — easiest)

Most popular channels have a one-tap preset. You don't have to remember channel numbers.

1. Tap the **Remote** tab at the bottom. Under **Select Source**, tap the box that feeds this TV (e.g. "Cable Box - Spectrum").
2. Look for the **Quick Channel Access** tiles. They usually cover Bally Sports Wisconsin+, FanDuel Sports Network, ESPN, Fox Sports 1, the major networks, and common news channels.
3. **Tap the tile** for the channel the customer asked for. The cable box tunes within 3-5 seconds.

### What to do (no preset — type the number)

1. Still on the **Remote** tab with that box selected, use the **number pad**.
2. Tap the digits — for example, **2-0-6** for ESPN.
3. Tap the green **ENTER** button (or just wait ~3 seconds — it sends on its own).
4. Wait 3-5 seconds. Cable boxes are slow. (Full walkthrough: **change-channel-preset.md**.)

### How you know it worked

The TV shows the channel's graphics — score bug, ticker, logo, whatever that channel uses. The preset button (if you used one) briefly highlights as "Now playing".

### What to say to the customer

"Coming up." If it's a sports channel and you know the game: "Bucks game's on now, just pulling it up."

### If it didn't work

- **Cable box didn't change channels:** the IR signal sometimes misses. Tap the same preset or retype the number once more.
- **Wrong channel landed:** just type the right number now — it will overwrite.
- **Preset says "channel not available":** the bar's subscription doesn't carry it. Offer an alternative.
- **Number pad won't respond:** try once more, then see **WRONG_CHANNEL_ON_TV.md**.

### Channel cheat-sheet for Wisconsin sports

- **Brewers** → 308 (Bally Sports Wisconsin+)
- **Bucks** → 40 (FanDuel Sports Network Wisconsin)
- **Packers (national)** → 5 (Fox), 9 (CBS), or 13 (NBC) depending on the week
- **ESPN** → 206
- **Fox Sports 1** → 219
- **NFL Network** → 212

### What NOT to do

- **Don't tap the preset repeatedly.** Wait 5 seconds between attempts — rapid taps confuse the cable box.
- **Don't change a TV that's already showing a game customers are watching** without asking. Find a different TV.

## Scenario 5: "Wait, none of my changes stuck"

**What this looks like:** you've been routing TVs for a few minutes and earlier changes have reverted, or the same TV keeps flipping back to the wrong game.

This usually isn't a bug — it's the system's **Override-Learn** feature trying to help, and sometimes getting in the way.

### What's actually happening

The system watches what bartenders do. If you regularly put the Packers on TV 7 every Sunday, it notices and starts suggesting "Packers on TV 7" for next week's game. Over weeks, this saves you real work.

But if there's also a **scheduled game** running (Scenario 3) and you manually override it, the two can fight: the schedule tunes the game it was told to, and your manual change gets recorded as a correction.

The system uses a 10-minute window: any manual change within 10 minutes of a scheduled tune is treated as a correction. After 10 minutes, it's treated as unrelated.

### Quick check first

If TVs are flipping back, open the **Schedule** tab. Is there an active scheduled game on the same TV? If yes, that's the fight.

### What to do

1. Open the Schedule tab (More → Schedule).
2. Find the conflicting scheduled game.
3. Either **Edit the TVs** (remove the TV you want for something else) or **Cancel the game** entirely.
4. Save.

Now your manual changes stick.

### How you know it worked

The TV stops flipping back. No more "why does it keep changing" frustration.

### If you're not sure what's going on

The Schedule tab has a **Scheduler corrections** card near the bottom. It shows recent patterns the system noticed — like "Bartenders moved the Bears game off TV 12 three times this month". If you see your TV listed, that's your hint. To make the correction permanent (so the system stops suggesting the bad assignment), tap **Apply** on that row.

### What NOT to do

- **Don't keep manually overriding the same TV ten times.** Every override gets recorded as a correction, and the digest becomes a mess. Fix the schedule instead.
- **Don't try to disable Override-Learn from the bartender remote** — only the manager has that switch. If you think the system is over-correcting, tell the manager.

## Scenario 6: "Two big games at once, same area"

**What this looks like:** Packers and Bucks at the same time, customers in the main bar want both, you only have two TVs in that section.

There's a feature called **multi-view** that splits one TV into a 2x2 grid showing four sources at once. It exists, it works, and most bars use it a few times a season at most.

### Quick version

1. On the Video tab, tap the TV you want to split.
2. Look for a **Multi-View** or **Quad View** button in the source panel.
3. Tap it — the TV switches to four small panels.
4. Tap each panel and pick a source for it. The audio comes from the upper-left panel, so put your "main" game there.

### When to skip multi-view

Multi-view is fiddly — small panels are hard to read across the room, audio only comes from one panel, and sports bars usually have plenty of TVs. Nine times out of ten, you're better off putting each game on its own TV and pointing the customer at the right one.

If you've never used multi-view and a customer asks: offer "I can put each game on a separate TV right next to each other, would that work?" It's faster and looks better.

## When to text the manager

Text the manager when:

- You tried two or three scenarios above and the TVs still aren't right.
- A whole section of TVs is misbehaving (see **WRONG_CHANNEL_ON_TV.md** — "everything is wrong").
- The iPad is frozen, the Guide won't load, or AI Suggest has spun more than 5 minutes.
- A customer wants a channel you know the bar carries, but preset and manual tune both fail.
- A scheduled game's status looks wrong and you can't tell what happened.

### What to include

1. **Photo of the TV** showing the problem.
2. **Screenshot of the iPad** showing what you were on.
3. **One sentence:** which TV, which game, what you tried, what's happening now.
4. **What you're doing meanwhile** — "Put the Bucks on TV 9 for now, customer is fine."

### Example text

> Hey, Brewers won't tune on TV 7. Tapped the cable preset and typed 308 manually. TV is on cable, channel doesn't change. Tried twice. Put it on TV 9 for the customer. Screenshot attached.

That's enough for the manager to fix it remotely or tell you the next step.

## If you get stuck, ask the AI

There's a small floating button on the iPad — usually bottom-right — that opens a chat with the bar's own AI assistant. Tap it and ask in normal English: "how do I put the Bucks on TV 7?" or "the Schedule tab is empty, what now?"

The AI knows these docs and the bar's hardware, and answers in a few seconds. It can't *fix* anything — it can only explain. If it tells you to do something and it doesn't work, fall back to texting the manager.

## A few things worth knowing

- **If you tap and nothing happens for a second, give it the second.** Especially on the Video tab when changing sources, and on the Schedule tab when AI Suggest is running.
- **The Guide tab is your most powerful tool for "find the game and put it on" requests.** Learn it before you lean on the floor-plan layout.
- **The Schedule tab pays for itself on game days.** Five minutes of pre-assigning at the start of an NFL Sunday saves an hour of running around.
- **Override-Learn is invisible most of the time.** You don't need to think about it. It only surfaces in Scenario 5 when something is fighting your changes.
- **You really cannot break this.** TVs are built to be switched thousands of times. Cable boxes don't mind being retuned. The schedule lets you cancel anything. Try things.
