# Lighting and Scenes — Bartender Help

**For:** the bartender working the bar tonight.
**Goal:** make the room look right for what's happening — trivia, the game, DJ set, last call, emergency.
**Time to fix:** under 10 seconds for almost everything. Most changes happen in well under a second.
**You can't break it.** Lighting is the most forgiving system in the bar. Every change is undone by the next button you tap. No combination of taps can damage a light. Worst case, you pick a scene you don't like and tap a different one.

---

## How lighting works here — 30-second primer

The bar's lights are organized into **scenes** and **zones**.

A **scene** is a preset — the manager saved the exact look of the room at one moment and gave it a name. "Trivia" might mean overheads at 30% and bar accents warm amber. "Game Day" might mean everything bright with accents in team colors. Tap a scene, every light moves to that saved look at the same time.

A **zone** is one area or set of lights — main bar overheads, patio, back room, accents behind the bottles. Each zone has its own brightness slider so you can nudge one area without touching the rest.

You'll mostly use scenes — faster, intentional, already tuned. Reach for zone sliders only when a customer asks for something specific in one spot.

## 30-second triage — start here

Pick up the **iPad behind the bar** and tap the tab labeled **Lighting** at the bottom. Then:

- **You see scene buttons with names like "Trivia", "Game Day", "Ambient":** good — find your scenario below.
- **No Lighting tab at the bottom at all:** jump to **Scenario 7 — no Lighting tab**.
- **Tab is there but says "No DMX controllers configured" or "No scenes configured yet":** lighting hardware is partially set up but not finished. Text the manager that lighting isn't usable and skip the rest.

## Scenario 1 — Trivia night, the host wants it darker

**What this looks like:** the trivia host is setting up and wants the room dim so people focus on the screens.

**Time to fix:** about 5 seconds.

### What to do

1. Pick up the **iPad behind the bar**.
2. Tap the **Lighting** tab at the bottom.
3. The most-used scenes show up under **Favorites** at the top — your "Trivia" scene is probably there.
4. If not in Favorites, scroll to **All Scenes** and find one named "Trivia", "Dim", "Mellow", or "Ambient".
5. **Tap the scene button once.** The room responds in under a second.

### How you know it worked

Overheads fade down, accents warm up. A small confirmation message ("Trivia activated") shows at the top for a few seconds. The scene button looks highlighted — that's the current one.

### What the customer sees while you fix it

Most scenes fade over 1-3 seconds — more like a sunset than a snap. Guests barely notice until they look up. Some bars' scenes snap instantly; that's set when the scene was saved, not something you control.

### If it didn't work

- **No scene named for trivia:** any scene in the "ambient" or "mellow" category will get you close.
- **Lights didn't change at all:** check the status pill at the top of the Lighting tab — if it shows "0/1 Online" or a red dot, jump to **When to text the manager**.
- **Tapped the wrong scene:** see Scenario 5. Just tap the right one.

### What NOT to do

Don't repeatedly tap the same scene while the room is mid-fade — extra taps queue up and can make the room flicker. Don't walk around flipping wall switches; at most bars with this system, wall switches are locked or ignored while a scene is active. Use the iPad.

## Scenario 2 — Lights came up at the end of a scene and it looks bad

**What this looks like:** the bar was set to "Ambient" all afternoon, then suddenly every light came up bright — usually a scheduled scene ending (like a 10 PM "Prime Time" brightening).

**Time to fix:** about 5 seconds.

### Quick check first

Is the room genuinely too bright, or did the energy change and the lighting matches it now? Sometimes a scheduled change is correct (kitchen brightening at close-out so staff can clean). If nobody is squinting, leave it alone.

### What to do

1. **Lighting** tab → tap the scene that matches the current vibe — whatever you had before, or a different one that fits.
2. Room re-settles in under a second.

The scene button you tapped is now highlighted as the current one. Same smooth fade as Scenario 1.

### If it didn't work

- **Re-tapped the old scene and nothing happened:** the system may still think it's active. Tap a DIFFERENT scene first, wait 2 seconds, then tap the one you actually want. Forces a fresh recall.
- **A scheduled scene keeps re-firing every few minutes:** the manager set up an automatic schedule. Text them which time it's firing so they can adjust it. Don't fight it by tapping over and over.

## Scenario 3 — Need brighter in ONE area only (Commercial Lighting)

**What this looks like:** the room overall is fine, but a customer can't read the menu in the back booth, or the cleaner wants the patio brighter to sweep. You don't want to change the whole bar.

**Time to fix:** about 10 seconds.

### Quick check first

This only works if your Lighting tab has a **Zone Dimming** section — named areas (Main Bar, Patio, Dining) with a slider next to each. Scroll past the scene buttons. If there are no sliders, your install only supports whole-room scenes — pick a brighter scene instead (Scenario 6).

### What to do

1. **Lighting** tab → scroll past scenes to **Zone Dimming**.
2. Find the zone matching the customer's area by name.
3. The slider has a percentage on the right (like "30%"). **Drag right** to brighten, left to dim.
4. Lights respond as you drag.

### How you know it worked

Only that zone changes. Other areas stay where they were. Customers in that spot notice; everyone else won't.

### What to say to the customer

"Better? I can crank it more or back it off — just say the word." Guests usually undershoot, so always offer to nudge again.

### If it didn't work

- **Slider moves but lights don't:** a backup wall dimmer might be set low, overriding the iPad. Tell the manager.
- **No slider for the area:** that area might be on old switched lights. Tell the customer "that section isn't on the iPad."
- **Brightened the wrong zone:** drag it back to 50-70% (usually normal). No harm done.

### What NOT to do

Don't slam to 100% — overheads look harsh at 100% and great at 70-80%. Start in the middle and nudge up. Don't change zones during a scheduled event without checking with the host.

## Scenario 4 — Emergency: fire alarm, evacuation, everyone needs to leave NOW

**What this looks like:** something has happened and the bar needs to be lit up bright immediately. Fire alarm, medical emergency, a fight you need to break up, a power flicker.

**Time to fix:** under 2 seconds. Fastest scenario in the doc.

### What to do (Commercial Lighting)

1. Grab the **iPad**, tap **Lighting** tab.
2. At the top, tap the big **All Lights On** button.

Every controllable light comes on at full within about a second.

### What to do (DMX-only locations)

Some bars only have the decorative/color system (DMX) and no Commercial overheads. Tap **Lighting** tab and find a scene named **All On**, **Full Bright**, **Emergency**, **Cleanup**, or the brightest scene available (often "Game Day" or "Daytime"). Tap it.

A DMX-only install controls accent lights, not overhead room lights. If the room is still too dim, the overheads are on a wall switch or breaker — that's a facility decision. Focus on getting people out.

### How you know it worked

The room is bright. Every fixture the system can control is at full output.

### After the emergency

Tap whatever scene fits the moment ("Last Call", "Closing", "Ambient"). The "All Lights On" state isn't permanent — replace it by tapping any scene.

### What NOT to do

Don't fumble with zone sliders — "All Lights On" is one tap, fast. Don't tap "Blackout" (DMX has one) thinking it'll "reset" things; Blackout means lights OFF — the opposite of what you want. Blackout is a show effect for DJs, NOT an undo button.

## Scenario 5 — Wrong scene fired by mistake

**What this looks like:** you meant to tap "Ambient" and tapped "DJ Set" instead. The room just blasted into bright purple strobes during a quiet Tuesday dinner.

**Time to fix:** under 2 seconds.

### What to do

Take a breath. **Lighting is 100% recoverable.** Whatever you fired, you replace it by tapping the right scene. There's no "undo" button because every scene fully overwrites the one before it.

1. On the **Lighting** tab, immediately tap the scene you MEANT to tap.
2. The room replaces the wrong look with the right one in under a second.

### What the customer sees while you fix it

A quick flicker between two looks — about 2 seconds of "huh, that's weird" before the room settles. Most guests assume the bar's lights are on an automated system and don't think twice. If someone comments, smile and say "the system has a mind of its own sometimes." Move on.

### What NOT to do

- **Don't panic-tap a bunch of scenes in a row.** Each scene needs a beat to apply. Pick the right one, tap once, give it 2 seconds. Rapid-fire taps make the room flicker more, not less.
- **Don't hit "Blackout" thinking it'll reset the room.** Blackout means lights OFF — the whole accent system goes dark. In the middle of service, that's worse than the wrong scene.
- **Don't refresh the iPad or restart anything.** The controller doesn't care what's on screen — it just executes whatever scene you tap. The fix is "tap the right scene."

## Scenario 6 — Game starting in 10 minutes, needs to feel like game day

**What this looks like:** the Packers game starts at 7. The room is set to "Lunch" or "Afternoon Ambient" and feels sleepy. You want Lambeau-before-kickoff energy.

**Time to fix:** about 5 seconds.

### What to do

1. **Lighting** tab.
2. Look for a scene named **Game Day**, **Kickoff**, **Sports**, **Prime Time**, or your team's name ("Packers", "Brewers", "Bucks"). Many systems use a green category color for game-day scenes.
3. Tap it.

### How you know it worked

Overheads pop up to a brighter level. Accent lights (behind the bar, under the railings, around the TVs) shift to team colors. The vibe changes — that's the point.

### What the customer sees while you fix it

A noticeable, intentional shift. Unlike the subtle changes for trivia, game-day scenes feel like the room is "leveling up" — guests look around and feel the place gearing up. That's a feature.

### If it didn't work

- **No game-day scene exists:** "Prime Time", "Evening", or turning all Zone Dimming sliders to 80-90% will get you close.
- **Team colors look wrong (Bucks instead of Packers):** wrong scene loaded. Find the right team's. If your bar only has one generic game-day scene, text the manager about adding per-team scenes.

## Scenario 7 — There's no Lighting tab on the iPad

**What this looks like:** no Lighting tab at the bottom — just Video, Audio, Music, Guide.

**Why this happens:** your bar's install doesn't include automated lighting, or the hardware isn't hooked up. The tab is conditional — it only appears when the system detects a configured lighting controller.

The lights at your bar are controlled by **wall switches and dimmers** — outside the scope of this doc. Every bar's switch layout is different and you probably know yours from shift training.

### What's NOT in your scope (any bar)

- **Breaker panel.** Section goes totally dark (not dim — DARK, no power): don't flip breakers. The wrong flip kills the kitchen, POS, walk-in cooler, or cable signal. Call the manager.
- **Burned-out bulbs.** Note in the maintenance log; don't climb on a chair during service.
- **Wall dimmer buzzing.** Worn dimmer needing replacement. Note it, don't touch it.
- **Burning smell or smoke from a fixture.** Emergency. Cut the breaker if you know which one (shift lead's call), evacuate that section, call 911 if strong.

### If your bar IS supposed to have iPad lighting

Text the manager: "Lighting tab isn't showing up — is the controller still online?" Use wall switches in the meantime.

## Scenario 8 — You see TWO lighting systems on the same tab

**What this looks like:** the Lighting tab has a "Commercial Lighting" panel at the top with amber/yellow buttons AND a "Lighting Control" panel below with purple/colorful scene buttons.

**Why this happens:** your bar has both kinds installed. They serve different purposes:

- **Commercial Lighting** (amber/yellow panel) — **general overhead lights** (room, dining, patio overheads). Affects how bright the room is. The "All Lights On" button and zone sliders live here.
- **DMX Lighting** (purple panel) — **decorative and accent lights** (behind the bar, under the rail, around the TVs, on the dance floor). Sets the *vibe* — color, mood, energy. Doesn't make the room brighter; makes it feel like a sports bar, a lounge, or game day.

### Which to use for which scenario

| Situation | Which panel |
|---|---|
| Brighter to read menus | **Commercial** (zone slider) |
| Trivia / dim mood | **Commercial** overheads + **DMX** accent colors |
| Game day | **DMX** team colors + **Commercial** general brightness |
| Emergency / evacuation | **Commercial** "All Lights On" |
| Last call cleanup | **Commercial** "All Lights On" |
| DJ set / dance party | **DMX** for effects; leave **Commercial** dim or off |

A good preset built by the manager will fire both systems at once — tap "Game Day" on Commercial and DMX both swing into team mode together. If your scenes don't, tap one on each panel.

## When to text the manager

Text the manager if:

- Lighting tab is missing when it shouldn't be.
- Status pill at the top shows "0 Online" or a red dot — controller is off the network.
- You tap a scene and nothing changes across multiple zones.
- Yellow banner says "No DMX controllers configured" or similar.
- A scheduled scene keeps overriding your manual picks.
- Zone slider moves but the lights don't (wall dimmer conflict).
- Burning smell, smoke from a fixture, or loud buzzing.
- Half the room is fully dark — breaker issue (DO NOT touch the breaker panel yourself).

Include: **photo of the iPad screen** showing the Lighting tab and any error banners, **one sentence** of what you wanted / tapped / saw, and **what time** it started.

### Example

> Hey, lighting controller shows "0/1 Online" at the top of the Lighting tab — none of the scenes work. Started about 15 minutes ago. Photo attached. Using wall switches for now.

## The floating "Ask AI" button

For situations that don't match anything here — "the customer wants the patio lit like a sunset" or "is there a scene for birthdays?" — look for the floating **Ask AI** button on the iPad. Type the question in plain English. The AI knows every scene name at your bar and can tell you what's available. It won't tap buttons for you, but it can save you scrolling through All Scenes.

If you got through this guide and the room looks the way you wanted, you fixed it. If the lights didn't cooperate and you texted the manager, you still did great — you gathered the right info and gave the manager a head start.
