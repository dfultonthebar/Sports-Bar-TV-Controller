# Music or Audio Problem — Bartender Help

**Use this when:** The music stopped. One area of the bar is silent. The music is too loud or too quiet somewhere. Or the wrong kind of music is playing.

**How long this should take:** Under 2 minutes for the common stuff.

**Promise:** You cannot break the system by trying any of the steps below. Every slider, button, and tap in here is meant to be touched by you. If something looks wrong after you tap it, you can always tap it back.

---

## 30-second triage — start here

Before anything else, take 10 seconds and **walk a few steps from the bar and listen.**

Ask yourself one question: **is music playing somewhere in the bar, just not where you expect?**

- **Yes — music IS playing somewhere, but the wrong area is silent (or too quiet, or too loud):** jump to **Scenario 2** (one area silent) or **Scenario 3** (volume wrong).
- **No — the whole bar is silent, nothing playing anywhere:** go to **Scenario 1** (music stopped entirely).
- **Music is playing everywhere but it's the wrong kind:** jump to **Scenario 4** (wrong music).
- **Music is playing but it sounds bad — crackling, buzzing, distorted, cutting in and out:** jump to **Scenario 5** (sounds broken).

If you're not sure, start with Scenario 1. Most of the time that's the one.

---

## Scenario 1 — Music stopped playing entirely

The most common cause: the music service paused itself. The music comes from an outside service called **Soundtrack** — it streams music made for bars and restaurants. Sometimes the connection hiccups and it pauses on its own. This fixes itself 9 times out of 10.

### Quick check

Look around. Are any TVs still playing sound? If the TVs have sound but the background music is gone, that's a music-service hiccup. Keep going below.

### What to do

1. Pick up the **iPad behind the bar** (the one you use to change channels and volumes — it's the bartender remote).
2. On the screen, find the row of tabs near the top. Tap the tab labeled **Music**.
3. Look at the top of the Music screen. There's a box called **Now Playing**.
4. **If Now Playing shows a song title and album art:** the music is "playing" but maybe muted somewhere — skip down to Scenario 2.
5. **If Now Playing is empty or says "Nothing playing":** tap the big **Play** button (a triangle ▶ icon) in the Now Playing box.
6. Wait about 5 seconds.

### How you know it worked

The Now Playing box fills in with a song title and album cover, and you hear music start in the bar within about 10 seconds.

### If it didn't work

1. Still on the Music tab, **swipe down from the top of the iPad screen** (like you're refreshing a webpage). This refreshes the connection to the music service.
2. Wait 5 more seconds and try the **Play** button again.
3. If still nothing, scroll down on the Music tab and find the list of **playlists** (squares with album art and names like "Sports Bar Energy," "Classic Rock," "Top 40").
4. Tap any playlist. This forces the music service to start something fresh.
5. Wait 10 seconds.

If after all that the bar is still silent everywhere, this is past a quick fix — go to the **Escalation** section at the bottom.

## Scenario 2 — One area is silent, but other areas are working

This is almost always a muted zone or a volume slider that got dragged to zero. Easy fix.

The bar is split into **zones** — separate areas that each have their own volume. Yours might be named things like **Main Bar**, **Patio**, **Dining**, **UpStairs**, **Pavilion**, or **Party Room**. Each zone has its own slider.

### Quick check

Walk to the silent area and confirm: is it truly silent, or just quiet? If it's just quiet, treat this as Scenario 3 (volume problem).

### What to do

1. Pick up the **iPad behind the bar**.
2. Tap the tab labeled **Audio** (it might be next to the Music tab).
3. You'll see a list of zone names — **Main Bar**, **Patio**, **Dining**, and so on. Each one has a horizontal slider next to it.
4. Find the zone for the silent area.
5. Look at the slider. **Is it all the way to the left (at 0)?** If yes, drag it to the right until it's about in the middle.
6. Look for a small **speaker icon** next to the zone name. If it has a line through it (🔇), the zone is muted. Tap the icon to unmute it.

### How you know it worked

Within 2 seconds of moving the slider or unmuting, you hear music in the affected area. The slider position now shows somewhere in the middle. The speaker icon doesn't have a line through it.

### If it didn't work

1. On the Audio tab, look at the very top — is there a **yellow / amber banner** that says something like "Priority active" or mentions a mic? If yes, someone left a microphone hot somewhere (open mic, paging mic, jukebox input). The microphone is forcing that zone quiet on purpose. Walk the floor — find an unattended microphone and either turn it off or unplug it from the wall. The banner should clear in about 5 seconds and the zone returns to normal.
2. Look for a **cyan / light blue banner** that mentions interference or RF. That means wireless interference (often from a nearby TV broadcast truck during a big game) is fooling the system into thinking a microphone is hot. Same fix: find any hot mic and turn it off. If nothing is on, this self-clears within a minute or two — you didn't do anything wrong.
3. Try sliding the volume slider all the way up to the right, then back to the middle. Sometimes the system needs a "nudge" to register a change.

If a zone stays silent after all that, the speakers or amplifier for that zone may be the problem (not the iPad system) — go to **Escalation**.

## Scenario 3 — Music is too loud or too quiet in one area

Just drag the slider. You can't break it. If the room turns out too loud, drag the slider back down. If too quiet, drag it up. The change happens instantly.

### Quick check

Which area? Listen first — sometimes "too loud" turns out to be the TV sound, not the music. Make sure it's actually the background music you're adjusting.

### What to do

1. Pick up the **iPad behind the bar**.
2. Tap the **Audio** tab.
3. Find the zone name for the area you want to fix.
4. Drag the slider:
   - **Too loud** → drag slider **left** (lower).
   - **Too quiet** → drag slider **right** (higher).
5. Listen for the change. It happens within about 2 seconds.
6. Adjust again if needed.

### How you know it worked

The volume in that area matches what you want, the slider stays where you put it, and the other areas are unchanged.

### If it didn't work

1. **Slider moves but volume doesn't change:** the zone might be controlled by an amplifier with its own physical knob (a backup volume control that's set very low). Note which zone, finish your shift, and tell the manager — this needs hardware adjustment, not a bartender fix.
2. **Slider jumps back to a different position after you move it:** something is fighting your change. Usually that means a microphone is hot somewhere and forcing the volume down (check for an amber banner on the Audio tab — see Scenario 2 step 1).
3. **Volume changes for the WRONG zone:** you tapped the wrong slider. Double-check the zone name. Names are written next to each slider — "Main Bar" might be third in the list, not first.

## Scenario 4 — Wrong music playing

The music service has playlists — pre-made lists of songs designed for different vibes. Switching to a different one takes 5 seconds.

### Quick check

What time is it? Some locations have automatic schedules (mellow playlist during the lunch hour, high-energy during prime time). If a playlist switched on its own, it's probably the schedule doing its job — but you can still override it.

### What to do

1. Pick up the **iPad behind the bar**.
2. Tap the **Music** tab.
3. Scroll down past the **Now Playing** box. You'll see a grid of squares — each one is a playlist. They have album cover art and names like **Sports Bar Energy**, **Classic Rock**, **Top 40 Hits**, **Country**, **Acoustic Mellow**.
4. The most-recently-played playlists are usually at the top of the grid.
5. **Tap the playlist you want.** A new song starts within about 5 seconds.

### How you know it worked

The Now Playing box at the top of the Music tab updates to show a song from the new playlist. The music in the bar changes within about 10 seconds.

### If it didn't work

1. **Tapped a playlist and nothing happened:** wait 15 more seconds — the music service sometimes takes a moment to grab the first song. If still nothing, swipe down on the screen to refresh, then try again.
2. **Can't find a playlist you like:** scroll the whole grid — there are usually 15-30 to choose from. If you genuinely don't have one that fits the vibe (e.g. you need a specific country playlist for a private party), tell the manager — they can add new playlists in the office.
3. **Playlist switches back to the old one after a minute:** an automatic schedule is forcing the change. Tell the manager which day/time this happens and they'll adjust the schedule.

## Scenario 5 — The whole system sounds distorted, crackling, or buzzing

**This is not a quick bartender fix.** Distortion almost always means a hardware problem — a blown speaker, a bad cable, an overheating amplifier, or an audio processor that needs to be reset. Don't keep tapping things hoping it improves; you'll just make the diagnosis harder for whoever fixes it.

### Quick check

Is it all zones, or just one? Is it certain songs or every song? Note this — it matters for the fix.

### What to do

1. On the **iPad behind the bar**, take a **screenshot** of the Music tab and the Audio tab (press the power button + volume up button on the iPad at the same time — same as taking a phone screenshot).
2. Note **the time right now** (e.g. "8:47 PM").
3. Note **which zones are affected** — write down the zone names.
4. Note **whether it's been like this for a while or just started**.
5. If the distortion is loud and unpleasant for guests, you can temporarily lower the affected zone's volume slider (Audio tab) to "tolerable" — that's a Band-Aid until someone can fix it.
6. **Text the manager** (or whoever handles tech issues) with the info above + the screenshots.

### How you know it worked

This step is about getting help on the way, not fixing the problem yourself. You "succeed" when you've sent the message with all the info.

### If it didn't work

If you can't reach anyone and the distortion is bad enough to drive guests away, you can:

1. Lower the affected zone's slider all the way down (mute it). Other zones stay normal.
2. Or pause the music entirely from the **Music** tab — tap the **Pause** button (two vertical bars ⏸ icon) in the Now Playing box. The bar will be quiet for a few hours until help arrives. Annoying but better than buzzing.

## Escalation — when none of the above worked

If you tried the right scenario and it didn't fix the problem, here's what to do.

### What info to gather (takes 30 seconds)

1. **Screenshots from the iPad:**
   - The **Music** tab (showing Now Playing).
   - The **Audio** tab (showing all the zone sliders and any banners at the top).
2. **The exact time** you noticed the problem.
3. **Which zones are affected** — write down the zone names. "All zones" is fine if it's everywhere.
4. **What you tried**, in order. Be specific: "Tapped Play on the Music tab — no change. Switched playlist to Classic Rock — Now Playing updated but no sound. Slider on Main Bar was already in the middle."
5. **What you're hearing** — silence? Distortion? Music in some areas but not others? The original music or different music?

### Who to text

The manager's number is **posted near the iPad** or in your shift handbook. Use that number.

### What to include in the text

A short message like:

> "Music is out in the Patio zone. Time: 8:47 PM. Other zones are fine. Tried unmuting the Patio slider on the iPad Audio tab — slider moves but no sound. Tried switching playlist — Now Playing changed but Patio still silent. No banners on the Audio tab. Screenshots attached."

Specific beats vague. "Music is broken" is hard for the manager to triage from home; the message above gets them straight to the answer.

### While you wait

- Don't keep restarting things — every change you make muddies what the manager can figure out remotely.
- If the bar absolutely needs music and you can't fix it through the iPad, ask the manager if they're OK with you playing music from a phone through a portable Bluetooth speaker as a backup. Not all locations allow this (music licensing) so check first.

## Things that are NOT your job to fix

These are hardware problems that need someone with tools and access. If you see them, skip straight to Escalation:

- A speaker is hanging from the ceiling by its wires.
- You smell burning electronics near the rack of equipment.
- The small black box on the equipment rack with the screen (the **Atlas audio processor**) shows a red light, an error message, or a blank screen when it should be lit up.
- Sound is delayed (you can see lips moving on a TV before you hear the words).
- Sound is in the wrong zone (you switched the Patio playlist but the Main Bar changed instead).

Touching the rack equipment, restarting hardware, or unplugging anything physical is **not** a bartender fix. Take the screenshots, send the text, and let the manager handle it.

---

## You did great

If you got through this guide and the music is back, you fixed it. If you got through this guide and had to escalate, you also did great — you gathered the right info and gave the manager a head start. Both outcomes are success.

The system is built to be touched. Sliders, taps, playlist switches — none of that can break anything. The worst case for any tap is "it didn't help, try the next thing." So try things. That's what the iPad is there for.
