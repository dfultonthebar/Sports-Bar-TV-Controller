# The Wireless Microphone Isn't Working

**For:** the bartender working the bar right now.
**Time to fix:** under 2 minutes for most of these.
**Promise:** you can't break it by trying any of these steps. Everything here is the normal way to fix it.

Work through this top to bottom. The easiest fixes are first, and they're also the most common. Don't skip ahead — most of the time you'll be done before you get past step 3.

---

## 30-second triage — look at three things first

Before you change anything, glance at these three things. They tell you which section below to jump to.

1. **The microphone itself (the handheld you talk into).**
   Are there any little lights on it, near where you'd put your thumb? Or on the bottom screen?
   - Lights on = it has power. Go to section 2 below ("Is it muted?").
   - No lights at all = the battery is dead. Go straight to section 1 below ("Dead battery").

2. **The silver or black box on the equipment rack** (the one with one or two short stubby antennas sticking up, usually mounted near the music gear).
   Are any little lights on the front of that box on?
   - Lights on = it has power. Good. Keep going.
   - No lights = the box lost power. Check that the power strip it's plugged into is switched on. If the strip is on and the box still has no lights, jump to the very bottom of this page ("Give up and text the manager").

3. **The iPad behind the bar — the one with the TV controls on it.**
   Tap the **Audio** tab. Look for a small panel that says something like "Wireless Mics" or shows a battery icon for the mic.
   - You see the mic listed with a battery icon = our system can talk to it. The problem is on the mic or the volume.
   - You see "no receiver configured" or nothing at all = that's normal at some locations, skip this step.

OK. Now work the sections below in order.

## 1. Dead battery (the most common problem by far)

This is the answer 80% of the time. Try this first.

### Quick check first

Pick up the handheld microphone. Look at the small screen on the bottom, or the row of little lights near where your thumb rests when you hold it.

- If the screen is completely blank and no lights are on, the battery is dead.
- If you see a battery icon with one bar or a red bar, the battery is almost dead and will die in the next few minutes.

Either way, swap the batteries.

### What to do

1. Find the spare AA batteries. They live in **[the drawer under the register / the manager's office / the back of the speed rail — your manager will have told you where]**. If you can't find them, text the manager and ask "where are the mic batteries kept?"
2. Hold the microphone with the head (the round mesh ball) pointing up.
3. Twist the bottom half of the microphone counter-clockwise. It will unscrew about one full turn and come off in your hand. **This is the normal way to open it — you can't break it.** If it doesn't budge, you're twisting the wrong way; try the other direction.
4. Pull the old batteries out. There are two AA batteries inside.
5. Put two fresh AA batteries in. **Pay attention to the + and − markings** stamped inside the battery compartment. The flat end of the battery (the −) goes against the spring.
6. Screw the bottom half back on. Don't overtighten — just snug.
7. Press the power button on the side of the microphone (or the bottom, depending on model). The screen should light up.

### How you know it worked

- The microphone's screen lights up and shows a full battery icon.
- Talk into the mic. You should hear yourself through the bar speakers within a second or two.
- On the iPad's Audio tab, the battery icon next to the mic name should now show full bars.

### If it didn't work

- **You hear nothing even though the mic is on:** the mic might be muted (go to section 2) OR the volume on the receiver might be down (go to section 3).
- **The screen still won't light up:** you put the batteries in backward. Take them out, flip them, try again. Pay attention to + and −.
- **The new batteries don't work either:** you grabbed dead spares. Try a different pair.

## 2. The microphone is muted

The handheld has a Mute button on it. It's easy to bump by accident when you pick it up.

### Quick check first

Pick up the handheld. Talk into it. Watch the iPad's Audio tab while you talk — there's a level meter that should bounce up when you talk.

- Meter bouncing = not muted, sound is reaching our system. The problem is volume (go to section 3).
- Meter flat, no movement = something is blocking the sound. Could be mute, could be the mic is too far from your mouth, could be the gain is at zero. Try unmuting first.

### What to do

1. Look at the handheld microphone. Find the small button or switch on the side of the handle, usually a third of the way down from the round mesh ball. It's often labeled "Mute" or has a small microphone icon with a slash through it.
2. If it's a button: press it once. If it's a slide switch: flip it to the other position.
3. Look at the mic's screen. If it was muted, the screen will change — usually a "MUTE" word disappears, or a microphone icon stops being crossed out.
4. Talk into the mic again.

### How you know it worked

- You hear yourself through the bar speakers.
- The level meter on the iPad bounces when you talk.
- The word "MUTE" is no longer on the mic's screen.

### If it didn't work

- **Screen still says MUTE:** you might have pressed a different button. Look more carefully — there's usually only one button on the side. Press it again.
- **Mic is unmuted but still no sound:** go to section 3 (volume).
- **No button or switch labeled Mute:** the mute on some models is hidden under a slide-off cap on the bottom of the handle. Slide the cap down (the same way you'd open the battery compartment, but only halfway) and you'll see a small switch.

## 3. Volume turned down on the receiver or mixer

Someone might have turned the mic volume down on the rack equipment earlier and forgotten.

### Quick check first

Look at the silver or black box on the rack (the one you identified in triage step 2). There are usually two small knobs on the front, one for each microphone channel. They're labeled with small numbers (1, 2) or names like CH1 / CH2.

If the knob is pointed down or to the far left, the volume is at zero.

### What to do

1. Find the knobs on the front of the silver box. There are usually two — one for each mic. Each knob is small, about the size of a dime.
2. Identify which knob goes with the mic you're trying to use. The screen above each knob shows the mic's name (often "Mic 1" or "Bar" or "Karaoke").
3. Turn the knob clockwise (to the right) until it's pointing straight up (the 12 o'clock position). **This is a safe starting volume — you can always turn it down later.**
4. Talk into the mic. You should hear yourself.

### How you know it worked

- You hear yourself through the bar speakers at a normal volume.
- If too loud or too quiet, fine-tune the same knob.

### If it didn't work

- **You hear nothing even with the knob turned up:** the volume is being cut somewhere else. Look at the **main audio mixer** (the bigger box with lots of sliders on it, also on the rack). Find the slider labeled "Mic" or "Wireless" and push it up to the middle.
- **You hear loud feedback (a squeal):** you turned it up too far. Turn the knob back down to the 9 o'clock position and the squeal should stop.
- **You hear yourself but it's crackly or cuts in and out:** that's a different problem — could be interference. Go to section 5.

## 4. The microphone and the silver box are on different channels

Sometimes the microphone and the receiver box get out of sync — like two walkie-talkies set to different channels. They can't hear each other until you re-pair them. The fix is called "IR Sync" and takes about 10 seconds.

### Quick check first

Look at the silver box's front display for the mic channel you're trying to use. It shows a name (like "Bar Mic 1") and below it, either a small TX symbol or a battery icon.

- If you see a TX symbol or battery icon = the box can see the mic. They're on the same channel. **Skip this section, your problem is something else.**
- If the display shows the mic name but no TX symbol and no battery, even though the mic is powered on = the mic and box are on different channels. Do the IR Sync below.

### What to do

This looks weird the first time but it works every time. The microphone has a little infrared (IR) eye, like a TV remote, and the silver box has a matching eye. They talk to each other to re-sync the channel.

1. Make sure the microphone is **powered on**. The screen should be lit.
2. Find the IR sensor on the microphone. **It's inside the battery compartment** — unscrew the bottom of the handle (the same way you'd change batteries), and you'll see a small black dot on the inside wall. That's the IR eye.
3. Find the IR sensor on the silver box. It's a small dark lens on the front of the box, usually between the display screen and the buttons.
4. Hold the open end of the microphone (the one you just unscrewed) about a hand's width (6 inches) from the front of the silver box, with the inside of the mic facing the box.
5. On the silver box, find the button labeled **Sync** (sometimes "IR" or "Pair"). Press it once.
6. The box's display will flash for about one second, then go back to normal. The mic's screen may also flash a quick "OK" or change to show the new channel.
7. Screw the bottom of the mic back on.

### How you know it worked

- The silver box's display now shows a TX symbol or battery icon next to the mic's name.
- Talk into the mic — you hear yourself through the bar speakers.
- The iPad's Audio tab shows the mic with a battery icon.

### If it didn't work

- **You pressed Sync but nothing flashed:** you may have pressed the wrong button. Look more carefully — the Sync button is usually labeled clearly. If you accidentally pressed Menu, the box's display will look different (showing words like "UTILITIES" or "RF"). To get back: press the button labeled **Exit** or **Back**, or just wait 30 seconds — the display will return to normal on its own.
- **The box flashed but the mic still isn't recognized:** try again, holding the mic closer to the box (about 3 inches away). The IR signal is short range.
- **Still not working after 2 tries:** go to section 5 (interference) — it might not be a sync problem.

## 5. Wireless interference (something nearby is jamming the signal)

If the mic worked fine an hour ago and suddenly sounds crackly or keeps cutting out, something nearby may have started broadcasting on the same frequency as our mic. This is especially common on game days at sports bars near stadiums — TV news trucks broadcast at high power and step on our mic frequencies.

There's a button on the silver box that finds a clean channel automatically. It takes about a minute.

### Quick check first

Listen to the mic. Does it sound like one of these?
- Static, crackling, or buzzing while you talk = interference.
- Cuts in and out every few seconds = interference.
- Works fine for 5 seconds, then dies for 5 seconds, then comes back = interference.

If yes, do the steps below. If the mic just sounds quiet or muffled, this isn't the right section — go back to section 3 (volume).

### What to do

This is called a "Group Scan" — the box scans the airwaves and picks a clean channel for itself.

1. **Power OFF the microphone.** This is important — if the mic is on, the box thinks the mic itself is interference and picks a wrong channel. Hold the power button on the mic until the screen goes dark.
2. **Leave the TVs on.** Don't turn anything off in the bar. We want the box to scan with all the normal bar equipment running.
3. On the silver box, press the button labeled **Menu**.
4. The display now shows a list. Use the small arrow buttons (up/down) to find the word **Utilities**. Press **Enter** (or the round button in the center of the arrows) to open it.
5. In the Utilities list, find **Group Scan**. Press **Enter** to start it.
6. Wait. The box will show a progress bar or numbers counting up. **This takes 30-90 seconds** — don't press anything while it's working.
7. When done, the box will display a suggested channel and ask you to confirm. Press **Enter** to accept.
8. Now do the IR Sync from section 4 above to push the new channel to the microphone (unscrew the mic bottom, hold near the box, press Sync).
9. Power the mic back on and test.

### How you know it worked

- The crackling/cutting out is gone.
- You hear yourself clearly through the bar speakers.

### If it didn't work

- **The display shows "SCAN FAILED" or just goes back to the menu:** the box couldn't find a clean channel. Try again — and this time, make sure ALL wireless mics in the bar are powered off (including any backup mics in the office).
- **You got lost in the menus and don't know how to get out:** press the button labeled **Exit** or **Back** repeatedly, or just don't touch anything for 30 seconds — the display will return to normal automatically.
- **You did the scan but the mic still has problems:** go to section 6 (escalate to the manager). The interference might be too strong to work around tonight.

## 6. The mic was dropped, the antenna is bent, or something is physically broken

If the microphone was dropped, has a visibly bent or broken antenna, or has any cracked plastic — **do not try to fix this yourself.**

### What to do

1. Take the broken microphone out of service. Put it somewhere it won't get used (the manager's office, or in the spare-mic box).
2. If there's a backup wireless mic on hand, use that one. The backup should already be paired and ready — just put fresh batteries in and turn it on.
3. If there's no backup, switch to a wired microphone for the night (the one with the cable that plugs into the mixer).
4. Text the manager: "Wireless mic [name/number] is broken — [describe what happened, e.g. 'antenna snapped off']. Using backup / wired mic tonight."

**Don't try to glue, tape, or reassemble a broken mic in the middle of service.** The internal electronics are sensitive and a half-fix can make it worse.

## Still not working — give up and text the manager

You tried all the above and the mic still isn't working. That's fine — there's a small chance the problem is something only the manager or the AV tech can fix. Don't waste more than 5 minutes on this during service.

### What to do

1. Take a photo of the silver box's front display with your phone. Get the whole front panel in the shot, including any error messages or status lights.
2. Take a photo of the microphone's screen (the small display on the handle).
3. Text the manager with all of this:
   - The two photos.
   - A short list of what you already tried, in order. Example: "Tried new batteries, checked mute, turned volume knob up, did Group Scan — still no sound."
   - What it's doing right now. Example: "Mic screen says full battery but no sound through speakers."
4. Switch to the backup mic or a wired mic for the rest of the night.

The manager can call the AV tech if needed. **Your job is done.** You did the right thing by trying the basics first and escalating with details.

---

## A few things worth knowing

- **You really can't break this equipment by pressing the wrong button.** The buttons all have "back" or "exit" options, and the box returns to normal after 30 seconds of no input. Don't be afraid to poke around.
- **The IR Sync is the answer more often than you'd think.** If a mic is "not working" and the battery is fine, try IR Sync before going deep into menus.
- **Game days are harder.** Mic problems during Packers games, Brewers games, or any major event at the stadium nearby are often interference from TV news trucks. The Group Scan in section 5 is the fix — keep this page open on the iPad and walk through it.
- **The iPad's Audio tab is your friend.** It shows battery levels and signal strength for every mic. Check it first before you walk over to the rack.

## If you've made it this far, here's what to bookmark

The two most common fixes are:

1. **New batteries** (section 1) — fixes most "mic suddenly stopped working" problems.
2. **IR Sync** (section 4) — fixes most "mic is on but no sound" problems.

If those don't work, do a **Group Scan** (section 5). If that doesn't work, **text the manager with photos**.

You've got this.
