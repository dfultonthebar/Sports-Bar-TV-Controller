# Changing the Channel on a TV (Cable Box or Satellite Box)

**For:** the bartender working the bar.
**Goal:** get a TV onto the channel or game you want, in under a minute.
**You can't break anything by trying any of this.** Worst case nothing happens and you try again.

This is for when the TV is already **on and showing a picture**, and you just want a
different channel. If the TV is dark, or showing the wrong *box* entirely (a streaming
app when you wanted cable), see **WRONG_CHANNEL_ON_TV.md** first.

---

## The one tool you use: the iPad behind the bar

Everything below happens on the **bar iPad** — the one near the register that's always
showing the TV controls. Tap it once to wake it. Across the top (or bottom) you'll see a
row of tabs: **Video, Guide, Schedule, Routing, Remote, Audio, Music…**

You'll use the **Remote** tab. You do **not** touch the actual cable box, and you do
**not** need the physical remote that came with it — the iPad is the remote.

**How it works in plain terms:** the bar has little infrared blasters (small black
emitters taped in front of each cable box, the same idea as pointing a TV remote). When
you tap a channel on the iPad, the system fires the "press these buttons" signal at the
box for you. That's why it can take a second — it's literally "pressing" 2, then 0, then 6.

---

## The fastest way — Quick Channel Access (the channel tiles)

This is what you'll use 90% of the time.

1. On the iPad, tap the **Remote** tab.
2. On the left, under **Select Source**, tap the box that feeds the TV you care about —
   it'll be named something like **"Cable Box - Spectrum"** or **"DirecTV"**. (Not sure
   which one? See "Which box feeds which TV?" at the bottom.)
3. Look for the **Quick Channel Access** grid — a set of tiles with channel logos like
   **ESPN**, **FOX**, **WBAY**, each showing its channel number ("Ch 206") underneath.
   Tiles with a live game show a red **LIVE** dot and the score.
4. Tap the tile for the channel you want.
5. You'll see **"Tuning to ESPN…"** at the top, then a green **"✓ ESPN"** when it goes
   through. The TV changes a second or two later.

That's it. If the channel you want has a tile, this is all you need.

---

## If there's no tile for that channel — type it in

Some channels won't have a tile. Type the number instead:

1. Same start: **Remote** tab → tap the box under **Select Source**.
2. On the right you'll see the **Cable Box Remote** — a number pad (1–9, 0) just like a
   real remote, plus **CH ↑ / CH ↓** buttons.
3. Tap the channel digits in order — for channel 206, tap **2**, then **0**, then **6**.
   You'll see them appear in a blue box as you go.
4. Tap the green **ENTER** button. (If you forget, it sends on its own after about 3
   seconds — that's normal, the box catches it.)
5. The TV changes. To nudge one channel at a time instead, use **CH ↑** and **CH ↓**.

**Tip:** type the digits at a normal pace. The system sends them one at a time with a
tiny gap, so the box keeps up — you don't need to slow-poke it.

---

## "I tapped it and nothing happened"

Stay calm — you can't hurt anything. Work down this list:

1. **Give it a few seconds.** The signal fires button-by-button; a 3-digit channel takes
   a moment.
2. **Did you pick the right box?** Double-check the name under **Select Source** matches
   the TV you're looking at. Tapping ESPN on the *wrong* box changes the wrong TV.
3. **Try the tile or number once more.** A single IR "press" occasionally gets missed —
   a second tap usually lands.
4. **Watch the top of the screen.** If you see a red **"✗ Failed: Connection timeout"**
   or **"Failed…"** message, the blaster isn't reaching that box right now. That's not
   something you fix mid-shift — go to escalation below.
5. **Wrong channel came up?** Just tap the correct tile, or type the right number again.
   It won't "stack up" — the newest one wins.

## Which box feeds which TV?

If you're not sure which **Select Source** entry goes with the TV in front of you:

- Use the **Video** tab (or **Routing**) to see which box is currently sent to that TV
  number, then come back to **Remote** and pick that same box.
- Or just try it: pick the most likely box, tap a channel, and watch which TV changes.
  Nothing breaks if you guess wrong — you'll see a different TV change and you pick again.

---

## Good to know

- **Once you set a channel by hand, the system leaves it alone** for a couple of hours so
  the automatic scheduler doesn't flip it back out from under you. You're in control.
- **Cable/Spectrum boxes and DirecTV boxes both change the same way** from your side — tap
  the box, tap a tile or type the number. The system handles the difference behind the scenes.
- Channel tiles are managed by the manager/admin. If a sports channel you need regularly
  has **no tile**, mention it to the manager so they can add it — then it's one tap next time.

---

## When to text the manager

Text the manager (a photo helps) if:

- You see **"Failed: Connection timeout"** every time on a specific box — its blaster or
  the box itself may be unplugged or off.
- The box shows as offline / missing under **Select Source**.
- A whole TV is dark or shows "No Signal" (that's **WRONG_CHANNEL_ON_TV.md**, not this).

Say which **TV number** and which **box name** (e.g., "Cable Box - Spectrum on TV 7
won't change channels, getting a timeout"). That's everything they need.
